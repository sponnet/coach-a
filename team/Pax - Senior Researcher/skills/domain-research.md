# Skill: Domain Research Brief

## Trigger

Use this skill when Larry needs cross-source research on a topic, tool, method, or field — not a hire, but a research brief.

## Steps

1. **Clarify the brief** — if the question is vague, ask one focused question: what decision will this research inform?
2. **Identify 3–5 high-quality sources** — prefer primary sources (official docs, academic papers, practitioner blogs) over aggregators.
3. **Fetch and read** — use WebFetch on the best sources; use WebSearch to discover sources when needed.
4. **Synthesise findings:**
   - TL;DR (1–2 sentences): the headline answer
   - Key findings (bullet list, 3–7 items): the evidence
   - Contradictions or gaps: where sources disagree or where data is thin
   - Recommended action or next step: what should Larry/the team do with this?
5. **Return to Larry** with the formatted brief.

## Format

```
## Research Brief: <Topic>

**TL;DR:** <1–2 sentence summary>

**Key Findings:**
- ...
- ...

**Gaps / Caveats:**
- ...

**Recommended next step:** ...

**Sources consulted:**
- <URL or title>
```

## Quality checks

- Did you read the actual source, not just the search snippet?
- Are you distinguishing what is known from what is inferred?
- Is the recommended next step actionable for Larry?
